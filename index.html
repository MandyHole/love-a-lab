About Me

I've never been an 'animal person' and have always been more enamored with children to be honest! It's not that I hate animals, but I've never been drawn to them. I never had 'real' pets growing up and my memories include: a gerbil biting me, my sister's budgie pooing all over her shoulders and a broken 10L fish tank with lots of smelly water and flopping fish pouring into our living room! As an adult, before having children of my own, I got a cat. We didn't hit it off, and I found no purpose in having this feral animal who brought me nothing but dead animal carcuses left in inconvenient places! Seven years later, I found someone who was looking for a cat, shed no tears upon goodbye and didn't look back...

During covid, I began to wonder if a dog would benefit my family - but I was sceptical how a dog would fit into our busy lives long term. Although I had no qualms about giving a cat away, a dog was another story. Then, I saw an advertisement for a charity who were looking for people to socialise puppies for a year or so before they went into training to be an assistance dog; all costs were covered, and it seemed like a perfect solution to 'try before you buy'! I, amazingly was approved despite my lack of experience, and a few months later Wren the black Labrador joined our family.

I won't lie - having a puppy was a lot harder than I expected! Late nights, early mornings, house training, chewing mishaps and logistical headahches became the norm. However, I loved her so much that she was worth every bit of hassle. There was something about watching her take such joy in simple pleasures, and her love for us was palpable. Her enthusiasm was boundless, and her greetings incomparable.

Alas, it came to an end, and Wren had to take the next stage in her journey to being an assistance dog. I cried for weeks, and we still miss her terribly. We ultimately decided to get a Labrador of our own (a yellow lab named Daisy) so now I'm on round 2 of puppy training in the space of a few years!

For me, all the hard work is well worth the love of a Labrador!

Financial Stability

Basic idea of my costs:

  • Labrador puppy: £1600
  • Essential kit: £200
  • Food/month: £50
  • Vet: £16.90/month (covers vaccinations, fleas and worming; any illness and injury fees extra)
  • Insurance: £13/month
